Field Test: Rift Lens 14 mm in the Exclusion Zone

Location: Zone Δ-7, Former Mining Town (Now Nonlinear Event Site)

Field Lead: Dr. Ilsa Grímsson, Aperture Stability Division

At precisely 0300 hours, the Rift Lens 14 mm was deployed under fog-dense, chronally unstable conditions. Our objective: determine how effectively the lens could maintain edge sharpness while peering into unstable parallel overlays.

Initial results were promising—vivid ruins and temporal phantoms captured simultaneously. One shot included three timelines stitched together in a single exposure. Our intern cried.

Despite one crew member temporarily being replaced with a 2D version of themselves, the lens never faltered. Results: crisp, layered, and deeply unsettling.

Test Verdict

A+ performance in unstable light-space conditions. Side note: time ghosts are not photogenic.

Field Test: Interdimensional View Camera in the Arctic Rift Caverns

Location: Subglacial Lab 4B – Arctic Rift Network

Field Lead: Dr. Sanjay Patel, Dimensional Optics Division

The Interdimensional View Camera was deployed 200 meters beneath arctic ice into a naturally occurring rift cavern. With its entangled sensor array locked to Timeline 7-Beta, we began capturing alternate geological formations and inhabitants—several of whom noticed us.

The camera’s Rift-Sync interface allowed us to cycle through dimensions in real time. Notably, Dimension 12-X featured a warmer climate and advanced penguins. They appeared mildly curious about our camera, then vanished.

Phase-conjugate lensing provided startling clarity. Image analysis revealed that one timeline contains a mirror version of our field team—one of whom waved. We did not wave back.

Test Verdict

The camera operated flawlessly. Recommend future deployment with diplomatic penguin translator.

All crew returned safely. Timeline residues cleared after decontamination soak.
