Our Origin

Paralax Optics was founded in the aftermath of a particularly successful science fair project, a cold-war-era garage lab, and a classified government mishap involving refracted antimatter. Originally operating out of a repurposed missile silo, the company quickly rose to notoriety when it successfully photographed a moment that hadn’t happened yet.

Our Mission

We exist to challenge the very definition of photography. At Paralax Optics, we believe capturing “the moment” isn’t enough — we aim to capture **possible moments**, **nearly-moments**, and occasionally **moments from other timelines**. Our work blurs the line between observation and participation in reality.

Our Team

Our core staff includes physicists, photographers, mathematicians, and at least one confirmed oracle. While our org chart defies linear description, all divisions are united under a single guiding principle: “If it works, it’s innovation. If it breaks, it’s a beta test.”

Every staff member is cross-trained in quantum theory, lens calibration, and emergency dimensional containment procedures. Team bonding exercises often involve escaping from pocket realities.

Why Trust Us?

We’ve partnered with three government agencies, two universities that don’t technically exist, and a shadowy book club with excellent funding. Over 10,000 successful field tests. Less than 14% result in mild anomalies.
